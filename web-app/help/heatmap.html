The "Heatmap" function plots the relative gene expression intensity levels of different genes across a population, or between two different populations. Different rows represent different genes (labeled on the left hand side). Different columns represent different subjects (labeled on the top). When plotting heatmaps for two subsets, subject names will be prepended with S1_ for subset 1 and S2_ for subset 2.

 High intensity is represented by red ("very hot"), and low intensity is represented by blue ("very cold"). Missing values will be plotted as black. Intensity values are logarithmic; each change in shade represents a doubling of intensity.

 Heatmaps can be used to quickly spot differences between populations; look for common colors within subsets but different colors between subsets to find interesting markers for further analysis.
